From the House of aeroKLE

From prototypes to production-ready systems

Trkshya (Aerothon 2024)

Has an aerodynamic profile that minipromizes drag, while advanced thermal management systems enhance efficiency under demanding conditions

ADDC 2.0 (ADDC 2024)

delivered breakthrough improvements in maneuverability, stability, and payload delivery, enabling seamless performance even in complex missions.

Vayu 2.0 (AeroTHON 2023)

Vayu 2.0 advanced innovation with a robust, aerodynamically refined design and integrated thermal management, ensuring reliable performance in dynamic, challenging missions.

ADDC 1.0 (ADDC 2023)

It demonstrates strong structural resilience and precise fragile payload delivery, offering durable, efficient performance across diverse missions.

Vayu 1.0 (AeroTHON 2022)

Aerothon 2022, the team’s first drone, established a legacy of innovation with a robust, aerodynamic, and thermally optimized design for reliable autonomous missions.

Vayu (DDC-Regular 2022)

This advanced dual-boom aircraft features a zero-drag fuselage and optimized stability to deliver the highest payload capacity yet, embodying innovation and championship-level performance.

AeroTHON 2021

This uniquely engineered, morphing-wing aircraft—powered by a 64 cc EFI engine—delivers 7 hp, 200 km/h cruise speeds, 28 kg payload capacity, and 6000 m altitude capability, making it a superior solution for reconnaissance, surveillance, payload drop, and cargo missions.

Jatayu (DDC-Regular 2021)

This redesigned, triple-tail Jatayu aircraft with tapered wings and a stronger fuselage delivered higher payload capacity and secured the team’s second championship victory.

ATOM (DDC-Micro 2020)

This lightweight aircraft with a rectangular split-wing design carried payloads many times its weight—an impressive 2 kg—earning the title “The Unsung Hero.”

Varthaman 2.0 (DDC-Regular 2019)

This innovative, lightweight, and strengthened prototype carried 5 kg payloads and soared to secure the SAE INDIA 2018–2019 championship.

Varthaman 1.0 (DDC-Micro 2019)

This highly stable polyhedral, out-tapered wing aircraft with a wood spar and conventional tail carried 1.5 kg payload, earning 3rd place in the SAE Aerodesign Challenge 2018–2019.

Dyaus (DDC-regular 2018)

This T-tail, rectangular-wing prototype with a former-built fuselage lifted 2.5 kg payload, securing 8th place in the SAE INDIA Aerodesign Challenge 2017–2018.
